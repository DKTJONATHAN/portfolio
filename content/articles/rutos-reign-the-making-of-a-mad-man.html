Rutos Reign: The Making of a Mad Man?

July 30, 2025 Opinions
RutoPoliticsKenyan Government

Is Ruto a mad man? The signs stack up: brutal protest responses, harsh taxes, corruption scandals, divisive talk, violence allegations, broken promises, and authoritarian moves


William Ruto, Kenyas President since 2022, won power promising to lift up the nations hustlers—everyday Kenyans fighting to get by. But in less than three years, hes sparked outrage, with some calling him a mad man. This isnt just talk; its a response to actions and scandals that make him seem out of touch. From violent crackdowns to broken promises, heres why the label fits.


A Blood-Stained Response to Dissent

Rutos handling of protests is shocking. Over 100 Kenyans have died since last year, including 38 in one day on July 7, 2025, per the BBC. Police under him face accusations of brutal force, mass arrests, and abductions, with orders to shoot in the legs fueling anger. Human rights activists call it tyrannical, one saying, The blood of young Kenyans is on his hands (The Economist, July 3, 2025). For a populist leader, this reckless disregard for life looks like a man losing control.


Economic Betrayal: Taxing the Poor into Rebellion

Rutos policies have turned supporters into critics. His 1.5 percent housing levy and 2.75 percent health insurance tax hit hard amid rising costs, clashing with promises to help the poor. This earned him the nickname Zakayo, after the Biblical tax collector (BBC, May 14, 2025). A Nairobi resident said, He promised to lift us up, but all hes done is take more from our pockets (BBC, July 12, 2025). Taxing without delivering benefits feels delusional, like a mad man lost in his own plans.


Corruption: A Trail of Greed and Scandal

Rutos past is riddled with corruption claims. He admitted in 2019 to illegally acquiring land for Weston Hotel, yet faced no real consequences (Wikipedia, July 11, 2025). Other scandals—like the Joseph Murumbi land dispute, KPC Ngong Forest fraud, and Muteshi land grab—show a pattern of alleged greed. Critics say this isnt just corruption; its a bold defiance of the law, as if hes above it.


Talking Loud, Listening Little

Rutos confrontational style worsens things. He personalizes national issues, like questioning why past leaders faced less backlash during protests (BBC, July 12, 2025). A political analyst noted, He talks too much and listens too little (BBC, July 12, 2025). Rambling while the country burns makes him seem detached, lost in his own world.


Shadows of Violence: The Jacob Juma Mystery

Most chilling is Rutos alleged link to the 2016 killing of businessman Jacob Juma, a government critic. Jumas tweets and activist claims point to Ruto, though unproven (Wikipedia, July 11, 2025). That such a suspicion feels plausible suggests a leader seen as ruthless, unbound by morals.


Promises Up in Smoke

Ruto promised affordable housing, healthcare, and jobs, but these remain unfulfilled. A student protester said, We voted for change, but all we got was more of the same (BBC, July 12, 2025). A Slide Toward Dictatorship

Rutos response to unrest—censorship, repression, mass arrests—hints at authoritarianism. The Economist called it a spiral of riot and repression threatening democracy (July 3, 2025). For a supposed peoples champion to erode freedoms so fast feels like a frantic grasp for power.


The Verdict: Madness Over Method

Is Ruto a mad man? The signs stack up: brutal protest responses, harsh taxes, corruption scandals, divisive talk, violence allegations, broken promises, and authoritarian moves. These arent the acts of a sharp politician but the chaos of a leader losing grip. Kenyans may debate the term, but the anger is real. Rutos reign is a storm of controversy, leaving many wondering if madness, not method, drives him. For a nation needing stability, thats a scary thought.
