TotalEnergies CHAN 2024: Everything You Need to Know About the African Football Spectacle

The TotalEnergies African Nations Championship (CHAN) 2024, set to ignite East Africa from August 2 to August 30, 2025, promises to be a thrilling celebration of football. Co-hosted by Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda, this biennial tournament showcases domestic league talent across 19 nations. Originally scheduled for February 2025, the event was postponed due to infrastructure delays and team withdrawals, ensuring a polished experience. Here’s your complete guide to the tournament, covering dates, insights, ticketing, ceremonies, hosts, stadiums, and the electrifying atmosphere awaiting fans.

Start Date and Overview

CHAN 2024 kicks off on August 2, 2025, and concludes on August 30, 2025. Organized by the Confederation of African Football (CAF), the tournament features players from domestic leagues, offering a platform for homegrown stars. With 19 teams competing across four groups, the event includes group stages (August 3–18), quarter-finals, semi-finals, a third-place playoff, and a grand final. Kenya alone will host 11 group-stage matches, setting the stage for a month of intense football action.

Key Insights and Facts

- Historic Tri-Nation Hosting: This marks the first time Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda co-host CHAN, serving as a rehearsal for the 2027 Africa Cup of Nations (AFCON) in the same countries.

- Tournament Format: Nineteen teams are split into four groups, with the top two from each advancing to the knockout stages. The groups are:

 - Group A (Kenya): Kenya, Morocco, Angola, DR Congo, Zambia

 - Group B (Tanzania): Tanzania, Madagascar, Mauritania, Burkina Faso, Central African Republic

 - Group C (Uganda): Uganda, Niger, Guinea, Algeria, South Africa

 - Group D (Zanzibar): Senegal, Equatorial Guinea, Sudan, Nigeria

- Past Champions: DR Congo and Morocco lead with two titles each, followed by Tunisia, Libya, and defending champions Senegal with one each.

- Prize Money: The winner will pocket approximately Ksh 452.8 million (USD 3.5 million), reflecting CAF’s investment in elevating CHAN’s prestige.

- Economic Impact: The tournament is expected to boost tourism, promote local talent, and enhance East Africa’s football infrastructure, with Kenya investing over Ksh 2.58 billion in stadium upgrades and a Ksh 1.6 billion hosting fee to CAF.

Numbers at a Glance

- Teams: 19

- Matches: Group stages, quarter-finals, semi-finals, third-place playoff, and final (exact match count TBD, but Kenya hosts 11 group games).

- Stadium Capacities:

 - Moi International Sports Centre, Nairobi: 60,000

 - Nyayo National Stadium, Nairobi: 30,000

 - Benjamin Mkapa Stadium, Dar es Salaam: 60,000

 - Amaan Stadium, Zanzibar: 15,000

 - Mandela National Stadium, Kampala: 45,202

How to Buy Tickets

Securing your spot at CHAN 2024 is straightforward with a fully digital ticketing system designed to prevent scams. Here’s what you need to know:

- Platform: In Kenya, tickets are available exclusively online via chan.mookh.com, managed by Mookh. Fans select matches, pay via M-Pesa or bank card, and receive tickets via SMS or email.

- Ticket Prices in Kenya:

 - Regular: Ksh 200 (~USD 1)

 - VIP: Ksh 500 (~USD 2)

 - VVIP: Ksh 1,000 (~USD 3)

- Ticket Prices in Uganda (proposed, pending confirmation):

 - Tier 1: UGX 20,000 (~USD 5.60)

 - Tier 2: UGX 7,600 (~USD 2)

 - Tier 3: UGX 11,400 (~USD 3)

- Availability: Sales began on July 23, 2025, in Kenya. Fans are urged to buy early to avoid missing out.

- Non-Smartphone Access: Those without smartphones can use a borrowed device or visit a cybercafé to purchase tickets and receive an SMS for entry.

- Security Measures: A four-layer protocol ensures safe stadium access:

 1. Initial visual screening at the entrance.

 2. Ticket authenticity scan via SMS code or digital ticket.

 3. Personal item screening.

 4. Guided entry to designated seating areas.

Opening Ceremony

The tournament kicks off with a vibrant opening ceremony on August 2, 2025, at the Benjamin Mkapa Stadium in Dar es Salaam, Tanzania (60,000 capacity). The event, themed “PAMOJA” (Swahili for “unity”), will feature the opening match between Tanzania and Burkina Faso, alongside fan engagement activities and digital storytelling to connect with African communities. This ceremony will set the tone for a month of exhilarating football.

Closing Ceremony

The grand finale, including the closing ceremony and championship match, takes place on August 30, 2025, at the Moi International Sports Centre (Kasarani) in Nairobi, Kenya (60,000 capacity). The event will celebrate the tournament’s champion and highlight Kenya’s pivotal role as a host. The third-place playoff is scheduled for August 29, 2025, at the Mandela National Stadium in Kampala, Uganda (45,202 capacity).

Host Nations

Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda are the proud hosts, with Zanzibar (part of Tanzania) hosting Group D matches. CAF President Dr. Patrice Motsepe has praised the countries’ upgraded stadiums, training fields, hotels, and hospitals. However, Kenya faced a setback, losing Group D hosting rights to Zanzibar’s Amaan Stadium due to inadequate training facilities.

Stadiums

The tournament will unfold across five world-class venues, each upgraded to meet CAF’s Category Four standards:

- Kenya:

 - Moi International Sports Centre (Kasarani), Nairobi: 60,000 capacity, hosting Group A matches and the final.

 - Nyayo National Stadium, Nairobi: 30,000 capacity, hosting key matches like Morocco vs. Angola and Algeria vs. Niger.

- Tanzania:

 - Benjamin Mkapa Stadium, Dar es Salaam: 60,000 capacity, hosting the opening ceremony and Group B matches.

 - Amaan Stadium, Zanzibar: 15,000 capacity, debuting in a major CAF event with Group D matches.

- Uganda:

 - Mandela National Stadium (Namboole), Kampala: 45,202 capacity, hosting Group C matches and the third-place playoff.

Training facilities in Kenya include Ulinzi Sports Complex, Kenya Police Sacco grounds, Utalii Grounds, and Kasarani Annex, ensuring teams have top-notch preparation venues.

The Thrill of CHAN 2024

CHAN 2024 is set to deliver unforgettable moments for football fans. Here’s what makes it a must-see event:

- Key Matches to Watch:

 - Kenya’s Group A Campaign (all at Kasarani):

   - August 3: Kenya vs. DR Congo

   - August 7: Kenya vs. Angola

   - August 10: Kenya vs. Morocco

   - August 17: Kenya vs. Zambia

 - Blockbusters:

   - Morocco vs. Angola (August 3, Nyayo Stadium): A clash of titans.

   - Algeria vs. Nigeria (Nyayo Stadium): A battle of African giants.

   - Senegal vs. Algeria (Amaan Stadium): A North-West Africa showdown.

- Fan Experience: The “PAMOJA” campaign encourages fans to bring team jerseys, national flags, and vibrant energy. Affordable tickets ensure packed stadiums, with local food and chants creating an electric atmosphere.

- Harambee Stars’ Moment: Coached by Benni McCarthy, Kenya’s squad, featuring talents like Mohammed Bajaber and Austine Odhiambo, aims to leverage home support for a historic run.

- Broadcast Reach: SuperSport TV will deliver live coverage, expert analysis, and highlights, making CHAN accessible across Africa.

- Safety First: A robust four-layer security system ensures a seamless and secure experience for all attendees.

Important Notes

- Olympics Confusion: References to the Paris 2024 Summer Olympics (July 2024) are unrelated to CHAN, which is firmly set for August 2025.

- Date Clarification: Some sources incorrectly cite February 2025; the confirmed dates are August 2–30, 2025.

- X Posts Context: Social media highlights Tanzania’s preparations and Uganda’s proposed ticket prices, but unverified claims (e.g., UGX 3,800 tickets) should be checked against official sources like chan.mookh.com.
