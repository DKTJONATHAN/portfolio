Former and Serving Officers Arrested in Kenya Amid Crackdown on Anti-Brutality Movement

Nairobi, July 30, 2025: Three prominent activists Jackson Kuria Kihara (alias Cop Shakur), Hiram Kimathi, and Patrick Osoi were arrested by the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) in a targeted operation against the newly formed Fighting Brutality and Impunity (FBI) movement. The arrests, occurring between July 29 and 30, 2025, signal an escalating clampdown on dissent following the group’s pledge to combat police brutality and state impunity.

Cop Shakur’s Arrest

Jackson Kuria Kihara, known as Cop Shakur, a former prison officer at Bungoma Main Prison, was arrested today, July 30, 2025, at Kahawa Law Courts while supporting fellow activist Patrick Osoi. Shakur, filming the encounter, challenged officers to provide clear grounds for his detention before being taken to DCI headquarters along Kiambu Road. No formal charges have been disclosed. This marks Shakur’s third arrest this year, following earlier detentions on January 15 for posting allegedly false claims on X about a cabinet secretary and President William Ruto’s involvement in abductions, and on March 25 in Naivasha for allegedly possessing a toy gun. His vocal support for the June 2024 anti-Finance Bill protests and subsequent suspension from Kamiti Maximum Security Prison have made him a target of state scrutiny.

Hiram Kimathi’s Arrested

Police Constable Hiram Kimathi, a co-founder of the FBI movement, was also arrested today, July 30, 2025, at Kahawa Law Courts. Kimathi, previously punished with a transfer to Todonyang Police Station on the Kenya-Ethiopia border for refusing a “shoot-to-kill” order during the Saba Saba protests, was taken to DCI headquarters. Authorities have not specified charges, but his arrest is linked to his activism against police abuses.

Patrick Osoi’s Arrest

Patrick Osoi, a former Kenya Defence Forces (KDF) Special Forces soldier, National Intelligence Service (NIS) officer, and US Army veteran, was arrested on July 29, 2025, at his home by heavily armed DCI detectives. Charged with preparation to commit a felony, Osoi was detained at Muthaiga Police Station. His arrest followed the July 28 launch of the FBI movement, which he co-founded to investigate extrajudicial killings and support whistleblower officers. Osoi, a 2027 presidential aspirant, has vowed to expose systemic abuses within Kenya’s security forces.

FBI Movement and State Response

The FBI movement, launched on July 28, 2025, aims to address police brutality and seek justice for victims of the June 2024 protests, during which over 50 deaths were reported. The arrests of Shakur, Kimathi, and Osoi, alongside that of activist Boniface Mwangi on July 21 for alleged terrorism facilitation, suggest a concerted effort to silence critics. The trio’s activism, amplified on X, has resonated with a public frustrated by unaccountable security forces and controversial government policies like the Finance Bill.

Ruto’s Legacy in Question

The rapid arrests have intensified accusations that President William Ruto’s administration is adopting authoritarian measures to suppress dissent. Critics argue that Ruto’s aggressive legacy-building, marked by heavy-handed responses to protests and targeting of activists, is pushing Kenya toward a dictatorial era reminiscent of its darkest periods. Public outrage continues to grow as the government’s actions fuel perceptions of impunity.

Ruto is overworking his legacy-building run to become the worst dictator Kenya has had.
